AGREEMENT

THIS AGREEMENT is made and entered into this ______day of ____________________, 1985, by and between QUANTUM COMPUTER SERVICES, INC., a Delaware corporation, having its principal offices at 8620 Westwood Center Drive, Vienna, Virginia 22180 (herein "Quantum") and LUCASFILM LTD., a California corporation, having its principal offices at 3301 Kerner Boulevard, San Rafael, California 94901 (herein "Lucasfilm").

WHEREAS, Quantum is the proprietor and operator of the QuantumLink Network and related software (hereinafter referred to as "Q-Link") providing Commodore 64 users and others access to certain electronic communications services, information services and software products (to include game and other entertainment software products) which Q-Link subscribers use in an interactive mode with other Q-Link subscribers; and

WHEREAS, Lucasfilm has begun research and development for and is the proprietor of an interactive entertainment software system known as the MicroCosm System (herein MicroCosm); and

WHEREAS, Quantum desires to retain Lucasfilm's research and development services with regard to developing an interactive entertainment software system and to obtain certain rights to market and make use of MicroCosm; and

WHEREAS, Lucasfilm is willing to provide such services and license certain rights in MicroCosm to Quantum in accordance with the terms of this Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the foregoing, the mutual promises contained herein, and other good and valuable consideration, receipt of which is hereby acknowledged, the parties agree as follows: 1. DEFINITIONS.

The following terms have the following respective meanings for purposes of this agreement:

1.1. "Advanced Object Set" means the set of objective behavior modules and imagery which Lucasfilm may develop as an enhancement to the Public Microcosm system release.

1.2. "Amiga Q-Link Subscriber" means a user of an Amiga computer who accesses the QuantumLink network by means of such computer.

1.3. "Avatar Personalization Kit" means the interactive software developed by Lucasfilm to allow a Q-Link Commodore 64 subscriber to customize the appearance of his avatar in the MicroCosm system.

1.4. "Baseline Home System" means the basic software residing on a Q-Link Commodore 64 subscriber's computer to handle interaction, communications and graphics for the Public Release MicroCosm system.

1.5. "Baseline Host System" means the software specified and designed by Lucasfilm and programmed by Quantum which shall reside in the Q-Link host computer system to permit interaction between and among multiple Q-Link Commodore 64 subscribers in the MicroCosm system.

1.6. "Basic Object Set" means the set of object behavior modules and imagery developed by Lucasfilm for the basic collection of common objects which shall reside on a Q-Link Commodore 64 subscriber's computer and in the Public MicroCosm System Release.

1.7. "Beta-test MicroCosm system release" means the preliminary baseline home system, preliminary baseline host system and preliminary basic object set.

1.8. "Commodore 64 Q-Link subscriber" means a user of Commodore 64 or Commodore 128 computer who accesses the QuantumLink network by means of either such computer.

1.9. "Conversion" means any version of the Public MicroCosm System Release programmed to operate on any computer other than the Commodore 64.

1.10. "Derivative Version" means any interactive entertainment system based on MicroCosm Story Elements.

1.11. "Documentation" means any material related to the MicroCosm system created by Lucasfilm to aid in the understanding, use or application of the MicroCosm system.

1.12. "Gateway software developers' kit" means the software which Lucasfilm may develop as an enhancement to the Public MicroCosm System Release to allow third-party software developers to develop software of their own creation for use in connection with MicroCosm system.

1.13. "Host database" means the software modules developed by Lucasfilm and Quantum under this Agreement which shall reside in the Q-Link host computer system to handle the necessary parts of the basic object set and advanced object set which must reside in the Q-Link host computer system.

1.14. "MicroCosm system" means the most recent complete version of the MicroCosm system, whether it be the Beta-test Microcosm system release, the Public MicroCosm system release or Advanced MicroCosm system containing additional features and components not included in the Public MicroCosm system release.

1.15. "Object Construction Kit" means the software to allow third-party developers to add new objects to the basic object set or advanced object set which Lucasfilm may develop as an enhancement to the Public MicroCosm system release.

1.16. "Public MicroCosm system release" means the computer program modules, programming and designs for the baseline home system, basic object set, baseline host system, and avatar personalization kit, turf personalization kit and system manager's package developed, written, provided or modified by the parties, and as generally described in the Lucasfilm's Proposal dated October 18, 1985, which is attached hereto as Exhibit A and incorporated herein by this reference.

1.17. "Q-Link subscriber" means any party who subscribes to the Q-Link network.

1.18. "Story Elements" means audio, visual, or audiovisual representations contained in MicroCosm which are presented to the user during interaction with MicroCosm and which are unique to or characteristic of MicroCosm. Story Elements does not mean or include any System Element, in whole or in part.

1.19. "System Elements" means computer software components of MicroCosm which operate on a Q-Link subscriber's Commodore 64 or a Q-Link host computer to present MicroCosm and to permit its interactive operation.

1.20. "System manager's package" means the software developed by Quantum which will reside in the Q-Link host computer and which permits Q-Link personnel to make such deletions, additions, or modifications in the MicroCosm host database as needed for routine operation of the MicroCosm system.

1.21. "Turf Personalization Kit" means the interactive software developed by Lucasfilm to allow a Q-Link Commodore 64 subscriber to customize the appearance of his turf in the MicroCosm system.

1.22. "Universe Construction Kit" means the software to allow third party developers to add new universes to the MicroCosm system which Lucasfilm may develop as an enhancement to the Public MicroCosm system release. 2. PROJECT_SCOPE_AND_COOPERATION.

2.1. This Agreement covers the research, design, development, and testing of the Public MicroCosm system release, and, if research and testing are successful (pursuant to the parties' mutually agreed-upon standards), the incorporation of such system release by Quantum on the Q-Link under license from Lucasfilm.

2.2. Quantum and Lucasfilm acknowledge and agree that the development of MicroCosm pursuant to this Agreement is an innovative, developmental, experimental effort, and that the success of such efforts and incorporation of MicroCosm on the Q-Link network requires both parties full and mutual good faith and cooperation.

2.3. Subject to the confidentiality restrictions below, the parties agree to share with each other all information and data necessary for the performance of this Agreement.

2.4. Lucasfilm agrees and acknowledges that Quantum may compensate Lucasfilm hereunder by means of a partnership or other legal entity, subject to the assignment and guarantee restrictions of Paragraph ____ below. 3. DEVELOPMENT_AND_PAYMENT_SCHEDULE.

3.1. The Development and Payment Schedule for completion of the Public MicroCosm system release is attached hereto as Exhibit B and incorporated herein by this reference. Exhibit B illustrates the parties' present expectations as to the timing, and is subject to mutually agreed-upon modification from time-to-time as the project progresses.

3.2. Detailed design specifications for the Public MicroCosm system release shall be completed by Lucasfilm and delivered to Quantum __________________. Quantum shall accept or reject such specifications within five (5) work days of the date of its receipt thereof. The parties may mutually agree in writing to modify the dates set forth on the Schedule at Exhibit B with respect to the delivery and acceptance or rejection of the detailed design specifications.

3.3. In the event that Quantum requests changes in the specifications or otherwise requests or requires changes in the deliverables or in the Development or Payment Schedules which in Lucasfilm's reasonable judgment require additional compensation to Lucasfilm, Quantum shall compensate Lucasfilm on a time-and-materials basis in accordance with written estimates submitted to and improved in advance by Quantum. 4. RESPONSIBILITIES_OF_LUCASFILM.

Lucasfilm shall provide the following to Quantum in accordance with Exhibit B:

4.1. Detailed design specifications for the Public MicroCosm system release;

4.2. Preliminary Basic Object Set components to be included in the preliminary Baseline Host Data Base of the Host Baseline System;

4.3. Preliminary Baseline Home System;

4.4. With the assistance of Quantum, integration of the preliminary Basic Object Set, preliminary Baseline Home System and preliminary Baseline Host System into a functional Beta-test MicroCosm system release.

4.5. Demonstrate the functionality of the Beta-test MicroCosm system release.

4.6. Assist Quantum in Beta-test of Beta-test MicroCosm system.

4.7. Complete final basic object set for Public MicroCosm system release.

4.8. Provide Quantum with the final basic object set components to be included in host database of hose baseline system for Public MicroCosm system release.

4.9. Complete final baseline home system for Public MicroCosm system release.

4.10. Complete avatar personalization kit for Public MicroCosm system release.

4.11. Complete turf personalization kit for Public MicroCosm system release.

4.12. Complete the host database for the basic object set with the assistance of Quantum.

4.13. Demonstrate the functionality of the Public MicroCosm system release.

4.14. Assist Quantum in testing the Public MicroCosm system release.

4.15. Complete documentation for Public MicroCosm system release.

4.16. Develop demo of Public MicroCosm system release to assist Quantum in marketing the system.

4.17. Assist Quantum in marketing the MicroCosm system.

4.18. Lucasfilm shall submit a monthly progress report to Quantum, not less than five working days after the end of each month signed by an authorized representative of Lucasfilm. Each progress report shall describe the status of Lucasfilm's performance since the preceding report, including the deliverables delivered, and the progress expected to be made in the next succeeding month. Each report shall describe Lucasfilm's activities by reference to the schedule of stages and tasks attached as Exhibit B. The progress reports shall identify and describe any unanticipated material problems, or delays, incurred or anticipated by Lucasfilm. The monthly reports shall continue until Beta-testing for the MicroCosm system is completed. 5. RESPONSIBILITIES_OF_QUANTUM.

Quantum shall provide the following to Lucasfilm in accordance with Exhibit B:

5.1. Provide at least one full-time host database programmer to incorporate the components of the project into the host system.

5.2. Complete the baseline host system with the assistance of Lucasfilm.

5.3. Complete system manager's package for Public MicroCosm system release.

5.4. Complete Beta-test of Beta-test MicroCosm system release with assistance of Lucasfilm.

5.5. Complete test of Public MicroCosm system release.

5.6. Announce release of Public MicroCosm system release.

5.7. Publish documentation for Public MicroCosm system release.

5.8. Publish software for basic MicroCosm system release.

5.9. Quantum shall, following release of the MicroCosm system, provide Lucasfilm with a written marketing report no later than the fifth working day of each quarter (i.e., reports to be submitted in January, April, July and October of each year, during the term hereof) containing the following data: (1) a description of marketing activities conducted during the preceding quarter; and (2) a review of projected marketing activities, sales, and projected sales for the subsequent quarter.

5.10. Quantum will maintain a high order of professionalism in the marketing and distribution of MicroCosm and to use its best efforts to maximize the royalties paid to Lucasfilm under this Agreement.

5.11. Quantum reserves the right to determine terms and conditions of sale for access to and use of the MicroCosm by its Commodore 64 Q-Link subscribers.

5.12. Quantum shall submit a monthly progress report to Lucasfilm, not less than five working days after the end of each month signed by an authorized representative of Quantum. Each progress report shall describe the status of Quantum's performance since the preceding report, including the deliverables delivered, and the progress expected to be made in the next succeeding month. Each report shall describe Quantum's activities by reference to the schedule of stages and tasks attached as Exhibit B. The progress reports shall identify and describe any unanticipated material problems, or delays, incurred or anticipated by Quantum. The monthly reports shall continue until Beta-testing for the MicroCosm system is completed. 6. CONFIDENTIALITY.

11.3. Each party shall bear all of its own expenses and disbursements incurred in connection with this Agreement and its performance hereunder, except as provided elsewhere herein. 12. SUBSTITUTE_NETWORK.

In the event Quantum elects to discontinue the Q-Link network, Quantum may substitute another network to support MicroCosm provided that any such substitution shall be subject to Lucasfilm's prior written consent, which consent may be reasonably withheld. In the event that any such substitution is approved, Lucasfilm agrees to assist Quantum in effecting a network substitution consistent with the objective of maintaining continuous operations of the MicroCosm system, provided that Quantum shall compensate Lucasfilm for any such efforts on a time-and-expense basis. 13.
